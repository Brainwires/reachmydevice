Your devices, everywhere — privately.

Open-source, self-hostable remote desktop. The host is pure Rust, media is end-to-end encrypted (DTLS-SRTP over WebRTC), and the rendezvous only ever relays ciphertext. Connect from a native app or straight from your browser — no install.

End-to-end encrypted

The relay never sees your screen

DTLS-SRTP secures video and the data channel peer-to-peer. The rendezvous does signaling + device registry only — it can't decrypt your session.

Pure-Rust host

No C toolchain, easy to trust

The host encodes with a pure-Rust pipeline (H.264 by default, optional AV1 via rav1e) and ships as a single signed binary. Verify it with minisign.

Browser viewer

Connect with zero install

A WebAssembly + WebGPU viewer runs in Chromium browsers: the browser decodes the H.264 track and renders it on the GPU. Just open a link.

Self-hostable

Run your own rendezvous

One small binary (or Docker image) is the whole server. Point it at your own domain and own your infrastructure end to end.
